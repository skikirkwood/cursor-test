# Contentful Value Messaging Framework

## Framework Context

Our value messaging framework (VMF) equips the entire field team with a unified way to communicate Contentful's value. It is grounded in Force Management's Command of the Message and provides a common structure to uncover customer pain, connect it to consequences and tie Contentful directly to measurable business outcomes. The goal is simple: move beyond features and consistently focus on business impact in every sales conversation.

Use this framework to guide how you position value in sales & success conversations. It will serve as the foundation for the sales assets you rely on every day, from discovery guides to proposal decks to Salesforce fields and differentiation. The concepts and language here will ensure we all tell the same, consistent value story that increases deal sizes and accelerates velocity.

## Key Terms

- **Value Driver**: The high-level business outcomes your target buyers care most about, such as increasing revenue, reducing cost, minimising risk, and improving customer experience.
- **Differentiators**: The unique, superior or defensible capabilities or attributes of your solution that are important to the customer, different from the competition, and provable, especially in relation to the customer's required capabilities and achieving desired outcomes
- **Current State**: The customer's existing situation or environment, including challenges, limitations, inefficiencies, or risks, that are preventing them from achieving their desired business outcomes.
- **Negative Consequences**: The risks or costs the customer faces if they remain in the current state (what are the implications of doing nothing). These could include lost revenue, wasted resources, poor customer experience, or falling behind competitors.
- **Future State**: The improved environment the customer can achieve with your solution, the vision of success that addresses the current pain and unlocks value.
- **Positive Business Outcomes**: The measurable improvements a customer will realize by moving to the future state. These tie directly to the Value Drivers and justify the investment.
- **Required Capabilities**: The critical capabilities the customer must have in place to achieve the desired outcomes. These are framed from the buyer's perspective, not product features. The key is to influence the required capabilities based on our defensible differentiators.
- **Metrics for Success**: The KPIs or benchmarks the customer uses to define what success looks like. These help you anchor value in measurable terms.
- **How Contentful Does It**: This is where you map Contentful's capabilities to the customer's Required Capabilities connecting the dots between their needs
- **How Contentful Does It Better**: How Contentful uniquely delivers the required capabilities more effectively than alternatives, based on our defensible differentiators. This highlights why Contentful is the best choice to achieve the customer's desired outcomes.
- **Proof Points**: Evidence that supports your claims, like case studies, data, ROI models, or customer logos, that demonstrate how you've delivered similar value to other companies.
- **Discovery Questions**: Strategic, open-ended questions used to uncover the customer's current state, pain points, goals, and required capabilities. These are critical for diagnosing value and driving deeper conversations.

## Overview of Value Drivers

### Revenue Growth: Accelerating Business Performance

Disconnected content systems and reliance on IT stall campaign launches and make personalization hard for marketing teams. Contentful brings all content into an API-first hub and gives marketers self-serve tools to build, test, and tailor experiences quickly. This agility turns slow rollouts into rapid releases and lifts both conversion and digital revenue.

**Target Personas:**
- CMOs and VPs of Marketing - focused on pipeline growth and campaign ROI
- Heads of Digital & eCommerce - tasked with increasing conversion rates and customer spend
- Directors of Growth or Performance Marketing - who need agility to optimize targeting and drive ROAS

### Operational Efficiency: Doing More with Less

Manual workflows, multiple CMSs, and developer ticket backlogs slow releases and inflate content costs. A single structured platform with a visual editor lets marketers publish independently while developers focus on higher-value work. The streamlined process cuts spend and shortens launch cycles dramatically.

**Target Personas:**
- Heads of Marketing or Web Operations - want to reduce handoffs and accelerate content delivery
- Directors or VPs of Engineering and Platform - looking to minimize maintenance overhead and reliance on custom builds
- Directors of Finance or Procurement - care about lowering TCO and consolidating vendors

### Risk Mitigation: Reducing Business & Technical Risk

Legacy stacks expose businesses to outages, traffic-spike failures, and security or compliance gaps. Contentful's cloud-native architecture delivers high availability, rapid rollback, and strong governance controls such as SSO and RBAC. The result is greater resilience and the freedom to innovate without fear of disruption.

**Target Personas:**
- CISO/CTO/CIO - ensuring compliance and protecting enterprise systems
- Legal and Compliance Directors or Counsel - who value governance and auditability
- Enterprise Architecture Directors or VPs - benefit from focus on extensibility and mitigating vendor lock-in risk

### Customer Experience: Elevating Engagement & Loyalty

Siloed channels, limited personalization, and sluggish pages erode engagement and push visitors away. Composable delivery, reusable components, and native personalization create fast, consistent, and relevant journeys across every touchpoint. Teams that adopt this approach see deeper engagement, higher conversions, and stronger loyalty.

**Target Personas:**
- CMOs and VPs of Marketing
- Heads or Directors of Digital Experience/UX - prioritize faster iteration and consistent design
- Heads of Personalization or CRM - care about delivering targeted, real-time experiences
- Heads of Support or CX Operations - value accurate content that improves CSAT and deflects support volume

## Discovery Questions

### Grow Revenue
- What prevents you from converting more visitors into pipeline or sales today?
- Which campaigns or segments would deliver the most upside if you could launch them faster?
- How do you measure revenue impact from content-driven initiatives today?
- Where are you missing revenue because content cannot easily tie into commerce or campaigns?
- What new channels or markets could unlock incremental revenue if content reuse were easier?
- How difficult is it to run A/B tests that tie directly to revenue KPIs?
- What is the revenue impact of delays in pushing new pricing, offers, or product launches?
- Which competitors are beating you to market with new experiences that drive sales?
- How confident are you in attributing digital content to revenue outcomes?
- What would doubling your digital revenue require from your content platform in the next 12 months?

### Improve Operational Efficiency
- How much developer or vendor time is spent on routine content changes each week?
- What manual steps in content production create the biggest bottlenecks or rework?
- How are content requests prioritized and tracked from idea to publish?
- Which legacy platforms or integrations add the most maintenance cost?
- How many separate CMS or database instances do you maintain, and at what cost?
- How often do developers get pulled off roadmap projects to support marketing edits?
- What content approval or legal reviews slow releases, and can they be automated?
- How easy is it for non-technical users to find and reuse existing content assets?
- What KPIs do you track for content velocity and cost per asset?
- If you could reallocate 20% of developer capacity, where would it drive the most value?

### Improve Customer Experience
- Where do customers encounter inconsistent brand or journey experiences across channels?
- How quickly can you personalize content for a new audience or locale?
- What customer-experience metrics (e.g., NPS, CSAT) are you trying to improve this year?
- How long do users wait for key pages to load on mobile and desktop?
- How well can you localize content across languages without duplicating effort?
- What frustrates your customers most in their digital journey with you today?
- How do you gather and act on real-time engagement data to refine experiences?
- How consistently is brand voice maintained across regions and third-party sites?
- How quickly can you diagnose and fix a broken journey or dead link customers report?
- What "dream" customer experience can you not deliver today because of tooling limits?

### Mitigate Risk
- Have you suffered downtime, slow pages, or failed deployments during peak traffic?
- What's the financial impact of an hour of site or app outage for your business?
- What controls are in place to roll back a faulty release or content error in minutes?
- How do you audit content changes and user permissions for compliance purposes?
- What are your uptime and SLA targets, and where do current tools fall short?
- Where does vendor lock-in limit your ability to modernize or switch components?
- How much technical debt exists in your current CMS, and what is the upgrade burden?
- Do you have documented disaster-recovery processes for your content platform?
- What security certifications or data-handling standards must your CMS satisfy?
- How prepared is your team to meet new regulatory requirements or privacy laws?
